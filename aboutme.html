Cooking

Now to my favorite topic. Food. When I first became pescetarian, I didn't even know If I could do it. I knew I loved chicken like crazy. I used to be a huge carnivore where I would order meat lover's pizza and buy 10 street tacos every other week to eat in one sitting. I was a hungry girl, and that never changed. But, my habits did. When I chose to leave red meat, I had no intention of ever eating it again. It took a lot of contemplation, of course. No street tacos? No more ribs? No more buttery steak? Yep. The whole sha-bang. But why? I noticed throughout life (please keep in mind that everyone's body is different and what has worked for me, might not work for you) I suffered with a lot of skin problems (acne) and I always felt so sluggish. Perhaps it was lack of a well-balanced diet but, at the time, I blamed it on the meat. After some good thought, I decided it was time for me to leave it behind and begin pursuing a new adventure with a new diet. I left red meat and within a few days, I already felt lighter. I felt more full of energy, and I didn't miss it at all.



My new diet consisted of chicken, fish, vegetables, and dairy. It lasted one year. I felt better. I felt stronger. Then, came along, college. During school, we did a fun little experiment for a psych class I had. Although, I don't remember the exact details of the project, I knew that it had to do with discipline. A project where we had to come up with a process (save money and have a certain amount by the end of the semester, bringing another classes grade up, etc.) where we had to write down a goal and report the steps we did during the semester to complete it. Even if we didn't complete it. I chose to challenge myself and cut another food from my diet. Chicken (play dramatic music...now). I loved chicken very much but I wanted to become more disciplined by eventually going vegan one day by cutting out one thing at a time. My process to leave chicken was simple and effective.


  • I wrote down my goal
  • I gave myself several weeks
  • I began by no longer eating chicken once a week
  • Each week that passed, I would eliminate a day that I would eat chicken until I ran out of days
  • My last chicken meal was McDonald's and I never looked back

After my chicken journey, I was officially a pescatarian. My diet now consists of Fish, shellfish, vegetables, and some dairy products. I feel healthy and strong, I feel like I am now forced to be more well-balanced because there aren't too many cheap fish options for take-out unless you want an expensive meal. So I have limited my spending, I enjoy cooking more knowing I will be making wiser choices with how I prepare my food, and I enjoy telling people what my diet is and seeing their confused reactions every time.



I don't plan to be pescatarian forever. My eventual goal is to go vegan, but until then, one animal at a time. Everyone is at their own pace and it's okay. This page is to help those trying to find a new way of life whether they need a slow transition to take steps to becoming a full vegan, vegetarian, or if you are just considering becoming pescatatian. The journey continues...
