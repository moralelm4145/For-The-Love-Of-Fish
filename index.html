What Is Pescatarian?



Pescatarians are like vegetarians. Their diet consists of fish, seafood, vegetables, dairy, and eggs.

Pescatarians are sure to stay away from any red meats and poultry. Basically, no meat except for fish.


A Brief History Lesson


The word was coined in the early '90s. Coming from the italian word for fish, pesce, and the word, vegetarian.

Some may spell it as pescetarian as well, which is also acceptable. Another description for it, is pesco-vegetarian.

The diet is a largely plant-based diet that consists of whole grains, nuts, legumes, produce, and healthy fats with seafood being the main protein source. Many pescatarians still eat dairy and eggs as well.


Wait..WHY do they do this?


There are many benefits to becoming pescatarian.

Below are a list of reasons why:




Health


  • Lower risk of obesity
  • Lower risk of chronic diseases like heart disease or diabetes
  • Less weight gain



The Environment


  • Raising livestock comes with a high environmental cost
  • Livestock contributes to 15% carbon emissions
  • Producing fish and seafood has a lower carbon footprint than red meat, poultry, and cheese production
  • The diets of fish eaters have 46% less greenhouse gas emissions



Ethical Reasons


  • Opposing animal slaughter
  • Inhumane factory practices

  • Humanitarian Reasons
